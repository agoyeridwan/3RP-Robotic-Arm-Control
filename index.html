Introduction

The 3RP configuration refers to the presence of three revolute joints and one prismatic joint in an articulated robotic arm. The three revolute joints enable the arm to rotate, while the prismatic joint allows for linear motion. This combination of joint types provides enhanced maneuverability and dexterity to the robotic arm. End effectors are positioned at the extreme end of the robotic arm they are used to perform specific functions. The end effectors used in this robotic arm is a gripper which grips objects within its reach.

Operating Instructions

Forward Kinematics

Forward Kinematics is a method used in robotics to determine the position and orientation of the end-effector based on the known values of joint angles in robotic kinematics. It describes the mapping from the joint space, helping to understand the overall configuration of robotic system.

Inverse Kinematics

This is the opposite concept to forward kinematics. Instead of determining the end-effector's position and orientation from joint angles, inverse kinematics involves calculating the joint angles required to achieve a desired position and orientation of the end effector. It is used for planning and controlling movement. It also allows the system to determine how the robot's joints should move to reach a specific target.

Procedures to use

  • Supply power to the robotic arm through the plug
  • Connect to the hotspot with the SSID (Robotic Arm) using the password mechatronics
  • Access the web application through your web browser by entering the IP Adress (192.168.4.1)
  • Click on the "Go to Controller" button in order to access the robot controller
  • Decide the operation to be performed i.e either forward kinematics or inverse kinematics
  • For forward kinematics, specify the joint angles J1, J2, J3,z
  • The controller then uses the angles specified to calculate the x,y,z co-ordinates of the end-efector. This triggers the end effector to move to the calculated position
  • Note that the value of z specifies the prismatic motion (i.e translational motion)
  • For inverse Kinematics, specify the x,y,z co-ordinates
  • The values specified are used to calculate the joint angles of the robotic arm
  • Click on move to position in order to move the joints of the robotic arm
  • Note: When on the controller page, you can access the manual by clicking on the funaab logo
